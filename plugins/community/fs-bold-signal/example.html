Why open matters

AI can finally design.
But the door is closed.

The proof arrived — closed

In April 2026, Claude Design proved an LLM can truly design: real design artifacts, not essays about design. But it ships closed-source, paid, and cloud-only.

Locked to one model

No agent choice, no self-hosting, no BYOK. The frontier capability is fenced off behind a single provider's cloud and roadmap.

Open Design sets it free

The same full design workflow — idea to prototypes, web pages, slides, and HTML video — open-source and running on your own machine.

Cloud-only vs. Open Design

Closed cloud design

  • Closed-source — one vendor, one roadmap
  • One model; no BYOK, no self-hosting
  • Your files live in someone else's cloud
  • ~$500 a month per seat

Open Design

  • Apache-2.0 — fork, self-host, go commercial
  • 21 coding agents, full-stack BYOK
  • Local-first: files, Figma, repos in place
  • ~$73 a month — about 80% less

What makes it different

A

Desktop-native

Design happens on the desktop. Local files, Figma exports, and code repos are directly readable; agents get full terminal power.

B

Plug in agents, don't rebuild them

Claude Code, Codex, and Cursor on your machine are already powerful. Open Design wires them into a complete design workflow.

C

It learns your taste

Every choice becomes design systems, preferences, and memory, so the next generation lands closer to what you want.

D

Open to the core

Apache-2.0: fork, self-host, and commercial use all allowed. Your work stays on your machine, in formats you own.
